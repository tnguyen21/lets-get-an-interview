Resume Principles

  • skimmable üëÄ
  • highlight reel
  • make it targeted üéØ
I honestly believe there are only 3 things you need to keep in mind to have a higher sucecss rate with sending in resumes. Make it really easy to skim, remember that it's your highlight reel and you only need to show the best work you've ever done, and that it should be targeted to the job description your applying for. Competitive jobs will have so many applications that they will have the luxury to pick someone with pre-existing competencies. Make it clear you're one of those people -- even if you don't feel like you are. You can and will become someone who does that job well, but only if you can get an interview and get the offer in the first place.

The Job Search üî≠

I'm a strong believer in keeping things simple and automated. The same goes for trying to keep an eye out on work that you think would be interesting to do, and would pay you well. I highly, highly recommend finding newsletters related to your career interests and subscribing to them. Even if you never read them regularly, it's nice to passively curate them. This way, when the time comes and you are looking for a job, you'll already have a stockpile of resources to reference for your search.
